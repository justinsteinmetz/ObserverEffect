Definition
When you observe something,
you change it.
This is the observer effect. Described by physicist Werner Heisenberg in 1927.
You cannot measure something without disturbing it.
Note

This applies to particles. It also applies to people.
It may be applying to you right now.

Documented cases
Where does it happen?
  • 01Speed cameras — drivers slow down, then immediately accelerate once past
  • 02Hawthorne factory, 1920s — workers produced more simply because they knew they were being studied
  • 03Wildlife documentaries — animal behaviour shifts near a camera crew
  • 04This room. Right now.

Language notice
How do we talk about
being watched?
When observation is the subject, the passive voice is the natural tool.
The action matters more than who performs it.
Active

Someone is watching us.

agent → action → receiver

Scientists conducted the experiment.

who matters less than what

Passive

We are being watched.

receiver → action — agent hidden

The experiment was conducted by scientists.

agent named with 'by'

Grammar — Passive Voice
The structure
subject + be (correct tense) + past participle + by + agent
Statement

The factory was monitored by scientists.

Cameras are being installed everywhere.

Question

When was the experiment conducted?

Who was it built by?
